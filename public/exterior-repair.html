Minor Exterior Repairs for a Perfect Paint Job

A flawless paint finish starts with a sound surface. Dependable Painting addresses minor wood rot, damaged trim, failing caulk, and wraps columns as part of our comprehensive exterior painting preparation.

Common Minor Repairs We Handle

Addressing small issues before painting prevents bigger problems later and ensures your new paint job lasts. We focus on non-structural repairs essential for surface integrity.

Minor Wood Rot Repair

We target small areas of rot often found on window sills, door trim, fascia corners, or siding edges.

  • Removal of soft, decayed wood.
  • Application of wood hardener to solidify remaining fibers.
  • Rebuilding the damaged area with durable epoxy wood filler (e.g., Bondo, PC Woody).
  • Sanding smooth for a seamless repair before priming.
  • Note: We do not replace entire boards or address widespread structural rot.

Column Wrapping

Protect and beautify deteriorating porch columns, especially bases prone to moisture damage.

  • Assessment of existing column condition.
  • Wrapping the existing column (often wood) with rot-proof PVC trim boards.
  • Creating clean, mitered corners and seams.
  • Caulking and preparing the new PVC surface for paint adhesion.
  • Provides a long-lasting, low-maintenance solution.

Siding, Trim & Caulk Repair

Addressing common surface imperfections before painting:

  • Filling nail holes, small cracks, and minor gouges in siding and trim.
  • Removing old, cracked, or failing caulk around windows, doors, and seams.
  • Applying fresh, high-quality exterior caulk for a proper seal against moisture.
  • Securing loose trim pieces where possible.

Exterior Repair FAQs

What kind of wood rot can you repair? ❯

We handle minor, non-structural wood rot, typically small spots on trim, fascia, or siding corners. We remove the soft wood and rebuild the area using durable epoxy wood fillers before priming and painting. Extensive rot requiring full board replacement needs a carpenter.

What is column wrapping? ❯

For porch columns that are deteriorating, especially at the base, we often wrap them with durable materials like PVC trim boards. This creates a new, smooth, paintable surface that is resistant to future rot and insect damage, providing a long-lasting solution before painting.

Are these repairs included in the painting price? ❯

Standard minor prep repairs (like filling small nail holes, basic caulking) are typically included in our painting process. More involved minor repairs like significant epoxy wood filling or column wrapping are assessed during the estimate and quoted separately or as an itemized add-on to the painting project, ensuring transparency.

Do you do major siding replacement or structural repairs? ❯

No, our focus is on repairs directly related to achieving a sound, paintable surface. We do not perform major carpentry, structural repairs (like foundation or framing issues), window/door replacement, or large-scale siding replacement. We can identify these issues during our inspection and recommend you consult a specialized contractor if needed before we paint.

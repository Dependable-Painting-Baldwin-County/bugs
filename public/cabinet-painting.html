Our Hybrid Painting Process: Quality & Convenience for All Built-ins

We combine the best of off-site and on-site work for a superior finish on kitchen cabinets, bathroom vanities, bookcases, entertainment centers, and more, with less impact on your daily life.

Step 1: Removal & Off-Site Prep

  • Carefully remove & label all cabinet doors and drawer fronts.
  • Remove existing hardware (knobs/pulls).
  • Transport doors/drawers to our dedicated finishing shop.
  • **In-Shop:** Thoroughly degrease, clean, sand, and prime surfaces for optimal adhesion.

Step 2: On-Site Frame Painting

  • Protect your space: Mask countertops, floors, fixtures, and adjacent walls.
  • Lightly clean and scuff-sand cabinet boxes/frames and fixed elements (like face frames, side panels).
  • Apply appropriate bonding primer to the frames.
  • Spray 1-2 coats of durable cabinet enamel directly on the frames/boxes.
  • Clean up the work area thoroughly at the end of each day.

Your Prep is Simple:

Just clear items out of drawers. Inside cabinets (kitchen, bath, bookcases), simply push contents back ~6 inches from the frame opening. Your space remains usable!

Step 3: Off-Site Finishing & Reassembly

  • **In-Shop:** Apply multiple coats of premium cabinet enamel (e.g., SW Emerald Urethane) via sprayer in a controlled environment for a flawless, hard, smooth finish.
  • Allow ample drying/curing time between coats for maximum durability.
  • Carefully wrap and transport finished doors/drawers back to your home.
  • Professionally reinstall doors and drawer fronts, ensuring proper alignment.
  • Reattach original or new hardware.
  • Final cleanup and project walkthrough for your approval.

Cabinet Painting FAQs

How does your process minimize disruption? ❯

We remove doors and drawer fronts to finish them off-site in our shop. We only prep and paint the cabinet boxes/frames in your home, typically over 1-2 days. This means significantly less mess, fewer fumes in your living space, and you can still access your cabinet contents and use your kitchen or bathroom throughout most of the project.

Do I need to empty my cabinets and drawers? ❯

You only need to completely empty the drawers, as we take those off-site. For the cabinets themselves (kitchen, bath, built-ins), you do NOT need to empty them entirely. Just push items back about 6 inches from the front frame opening. We carefully mask off the cabinet interiors to protect your contents while we paint the frames on-site.

How long does the cabinet painting process take? ❯

Most standard-sized kitchen cabinet projects are completed start-to-finish in about 4-6 working days. Bathroom vanities or smaller built-ins may be quicker. This includes removal, off-site finishing & curing time for doors/drawers, 1-2 days of on-site work for the frames, and final reassembly.

What kind of paint do you use for cabinets? ❯

We use premium, high-adhesion primers and durable cabinet coatings specifically designed for high-traffic areas, such as Sherwin-Williams Emerald® Urethane Trim Enamel or similar cabinet-grade lacquers. These are applied via professional HVLP sprayer for a hard, smooth, factory-like finish that resists chipping, scuffing, and daily wear in kitchens, bathrooms, and living areas.

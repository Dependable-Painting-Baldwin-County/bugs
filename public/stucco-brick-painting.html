The Right Way to Paint Stucco & Brick

Why Paint Masonry?

Painting offers several benefits:

  • Modern Aesthetics: Instantly updates outdated brick or stucco colors.
  • Water Resistance: Seals porous surfaces, preventing moisture damage and efflorescence (white powdery stains).
  • Uniform Appearance: Covers patches, repairs, and old stains for a clean look.
  • Enhanced Durability: Quality coatings protect masonry from weathering and wear.

Our Masonry Painting Process

Painting brick and stucco requires specific steps:

  • Cleaning: Power washing to remove dirt, mildew, and loose material.
  • Repair: Filling cracks and patching damaged areas with appropriate masonry compounds.
  • Priming: Applying specialized masonry conditioners/primers (like Loxon®) to ensure adhesion and seal the surface.
  • Top Coat Application: Applying 2 coats of breathable, durable masonry paint or elastomeric coating for maximum protection.

Choosing the Right Coating

We use products designed for masonry:

  • Masonry Paints (e.g., Loxon XP®): Offer excellent adhesion, durability, and breathability, preventing trapped moisture.
  • Elastomeric Coatings: Thicker coatings that bridge hairline cracks, providing superior waterproofing and flexibility. Ideal for stucco prone to minor cracking.
  • Brick Stains: Penetrate the brick for a natural look while still providing protection (less common for full color changes).

Stucco & Brick Painting FAQs

Why paint brick or stucco? ❯

Painting masonry updates its look, seals porous surfaces against moisture intrusion, and can cover old stains or repairs. Specialized coatings allow the masonry to breathe, preventing trapped moisture issues.

What kind of paint do you use on stucco/brick? ❯

We use high-quality masonry primers and topcoats like Sherwin-Williams Loxon® (conditioners, primers, paints) or elastomeric coatings which bridge hairline cracks and offer superior weather protection while allowing breathability.

Do you repair cracks in stucco before painting? ❯

Yes, proper prep is essential. Our process includes thoroughly cleaning the surface and repairing any cracks or damage with appropriate masonry caulks or patching compounds before applying primer and paint.

How long does painted brick or stucco last? ❯

With meticulous preparation and the use of high-quality masonry coatings designed for our coastal Alabama climate, a professionally painted brick or stucco finish can last 10-15 years or even longer.

The Right Approach for Every Metal Type

Crucial Prep & Rust Treatment

Proper surface preparation is non-negotiable for metal:

  • Cleaning: Removing dirt, grease, oils, and contaminants.
  • Rust Removal: Mechanical removal (wire brushing, sanding) of loose rust.
  • Rust Conversion/Inhibition: Treating firm, adherent rust with rust converters or specialized rust-inhibitive primers.
  • Abrasion: Lightly sanding or abrading smooth surfaces (like aluminum or galvanized steel) to create a profile for primer adhesion.

Priming for Adhesion & Protection

Different metals require different primers:

  • Ferrous Metals (Steel, Iron): Rust-inhibitive primers (e.g., Sherwin-Williams Pro Industrial Pro-Cryl Universal Primer) are essential.
  • Non-Ferrous Metals (Aluminum, Galvanized): Require specific bonding primers (e.g., SW DTM Bonding Primer) to ensure topcoat adhesion.
  • Previously Painted Metal: Spot-priming bare areas and using a compatible primer system.

Durable Sherwin-Williams Topcoats

We select the best finish coat for the job:

  • DTM (Direct-To-Metal) Acrylic Coatings: Versatile, corrosion-resistant finish suitable for many applications (railings, columns).
  • Industrial Enamels: Provide a hard, durable finish for high-traffic or demanding environments.
  • Specialty Coatings: Including high-heat paints for specific applications or specific roof coatings.
  • Color Matching: Wide range of colors available to match existing elements or create a new look.

Metal Painting FAQs

Can you paint rusty metal? ❯

Yes, but proper preparation is crucial. We mechanically remove loose rust (wire brushing, sanding) and treat remaining firm rust with a rust-inhibitive primer (like Sherwin-Williams Kem Kromik Universal Metal Primer) before applying topcoats. This ensures the new paint adheres and significantly slows down further corrosion.

What kind of paint do you use on metal? ❯

We primarily use high-quality Sherwin-Williams Direct-to-Metal (DTM) Acrylic primers and paints (like Pro Industrial DTM Acrylic), Industrial Enamels (like Urethane Alkyd Enamel), or other specialized coatings (like Macropoxy for severe environments) depending on the type of metal (steel, aluminum, galvanized) and its exposure conditions.

Do you need to prime aluminum or galvanized metal? ❯

Absolutely. Non-ferrous metals like aluminum and galvanized steel have smooth surfaces that paint doesn't easily stick to. They require specific bonding primers (like Sherwin-Williams DTM Bonding Primer) after proper cleaning and often light abrasion (scuff sanding) to ensure the topcoat adheres properly and avoids peeling.

How long will paint last on exterior metal? ❯

With thorough preparation (meticulous cleaning, complete rust treatment, appropriate priming system) and the use of high-quality industrial coatings, professionally painted exterior metal surfaces like railings or columns can last 5-10 years or more, depending heavily on the coastal environmental factors (salt spray, sun exposure) and physical wear.

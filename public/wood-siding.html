Our Exterior Wood Painting Process

Wash & Clean

Pressure wash to remove mildew, chalk & contaminants for proper adhesion.

Scrape & Sand

Loose paint removed and edges feather‑sand for a smooth transition.

Repair & Fill

Minor wood defects filled with epoxy; significant rot flagged before coating.

Prime Bare Wood

Oil or stain‑blocking primer seals tannins & locks in adhesion on exposed areas.

Premium Exterior Coatings

Top-tier Sherwin-Williams systems (Duration®, Emerald®) resist UV, moisture & peeling while allowing the substrate to breathe.

Wood Painting FAQs

How long will it last?

Typically 7–10 years; gentle annual washing can extend performance beyond a decade.

Do you fix rot?

We handle minor repairs & fillers. Significant rot is identified so you can approve replacement first.

Why prime bare wood?

Primer blocks tannins, ensures even sheen & prevents premature peeling or discoloration.

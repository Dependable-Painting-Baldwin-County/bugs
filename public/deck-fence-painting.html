Protecting Your Outdoor Living Spaces

Essential Cleaning & Prep

Proper surface preparation is key for longevity:

  • Power Washing: Removes dirt, grime, mildew, algae, and failing coatings.
  • Sanding: Smooths rough spots, removes splinters, and opens wood pores for better penetration/adhesion.
  • Repairs: Securing loose boards/pickets, setting popped nails/screws.
  • Brightening (Optional): Restores wood's natural color before staining.

Stain vs. Paint: Choosing Your Finish

We help you select the best option:

  • Stains (Transparent, Semi-Transparent, Solid): Penetrate wood, showcasing grain (transparent/semi) or providing uniform color (solid). Generally require more frequent reapplication.
  • Paints (Solid Color): Form a protective film on the surface. Offer wider color choices and potentially longer protection, but require more prep if peeling occurs.
  • Deck Resurfacers: Thicker coatings designed to fill cracks and restore heavily worn decks (specific use case).

Our Application Process

Ensuring an even, protective coat:

  • Careful Application: Using brushes, rollers, or sprayers appropriate for the product and surface.
  • Even Coverage: Avoiding drips, runs, and lap marks for a professional look.
  • Protecting Surroundings: Masking adjacent surfaces (house siding, concrete) as needed.
  • Thorough Cleanup: Leaving your property clean upon completion.

Deck & Fence Finishing FAQs

How often should I stain or paint my deck/fence? ❯

In Alabama's climate, decks typically need re-staining every 1-3 years (depending on stain type and sun exposure). Fences may last 3-5 years. High-quality solid stains or paints can last longer than semi-transparent stains. Regular cleaning helps extend the life of the finish.

Is staining or painting better for my deck? ❯

Stain penetrates the wood, highlighting grain (especially semi-transparent), while paint forms a surface film. Stains generally require more frequent maintenance but are often easier to reapply without extensive scraping. Paint offers more color options and potentially longer protection against UV/moisture but requires more prep (scraping/sanding) if it starts peeling. We can discuss the pros and cons for your specific situation.

Do you replace damaged deck boards or fence pickets? ❯

Our standard prep process includes minor repairs like securing loose boards or pickets and setting popped nails/screws. For significant rot, structural damage, or boards/pickets needing full replacement, we recommend consulting a carpenter or deck repair specialist before we proceed with the finishing.

What prep work is needed before finishing a deck or fence? ❯

Thorough cleaning (usually power washing) is essential to remove dirt, mildew, algae, and any loose/failing previous coatings. Depending on the wood condition and desired finish, sanding may also be necessary to ensure smoothness and proper adhesion or penetration of the new coating. Proper prep is the most critical step for a lasting finish.
